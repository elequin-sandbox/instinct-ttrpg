# Act Tactics — Boon Design

Boon cards have unique anatomy and rules. Always use this skill when working on Boons — do not apply general class card design rules without checking for Boon-specific differences.

---

## What Boons Are

Boons are **permanently in the player's deck from character creation.** They represent positive character qualities — not abilities, but truths about who the character is.

**Core rules (locked):**
- Passive OR active effect — never both
- Always includes a fill-in blank (Origin stem or Anchor stem)
- Stems are soft gates — suggest narration without enforcing it
- Trigger when the player narrates a fictional connection
- Subject to RNG draw (in the deck, not permanently visible)

## Boon Card Anatomy

Boon structure differs from class ability cards:

```
[Card Name]                         [Boon]
─────────────────────────────────────
[NO flavor text — stem takes this space]
─────────────────────────────────────
EFFECT
[Trigger sentence, italic — one line]

Choose one:  ← or passive text, or Luck Check line
**Bold Verb** — brief description
**Bold Verb** — brief description
**Bold Verb** — brief description

[Luck Check pill — if roll needed]
─────────────────────────────────────
[Origin / Anchor label]
[Stem sentence, italic]
[_________________________]
[__________________]
─────────────────────────────────────
[Boon · Base]                    [—]
```

**Key differences from class cards:**
- No flavor text (stem replaces it — the two compete for space)
- No failure line
- No strong roll section
- Cost pip is `—`
- Writing box is the emotional center of the card

## Framework A — Open Narration + Luck Check

**Use when:** The creative act is the mechanic — player improvisation is interesting, not choice between options. Best for socially variable or discovery-based effects.

```
[Trigger condition, italic], [what player does narratively].
Make a [Luck Check] — [GM adjudicates result].
```

**Luck Check mechanics:** d6, exploding on 6s · No stat applied · Boost dice eligible · On miss: narration window closes, scene moves on.

**Primary examples:** Lucky Break, Known Face.

---

## Framework B — PbtA Question List

**Use when:** The card's power is information — player tries to understand the scene, not change it. Three questions is the sweet spot.

```
[Trigger or passive condition], ask one:
[Question 1?]
[Question 2?]
[Question 3?]
The GM answers honestly.
```

**Strict standard:** Only use this if questions are clearly superior to verb choices — not just viable. Primary example: The Quiet Eye.

**Key rule:** Questions must be phrased as questions. The GM must answer honestly — hard commitment.

---

## Framework C — Choose One (CRPG Verb Syntax)

**Use when:** The interesting choice is HOW the character engages. Default for most Boons.

```
[Trigger or identity declaration],
Choose one:
**Bold Verb** — brief description
**Bold Verb** — brief description
**Bold Verb** — brief description
```

**Rules:** Three options — not two, not four. At least two options work across combat, social, and exploration. Options must be meaningfully distinct (different vectors). Three vectors model: self / party / scene.

---

## CRPG Verb Syntax

| Flavor | Example | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| Action verb | **Push Through**, **Find the Root** | Tactical, physical, skill-based |
| Named quality | **Callous Indifference**, **Suspected As Much** | Character-reveal, emotional register |

Description after `—` is direction, not instruction. One clause. Hints at fictional outcome without capping what GM can award.

## Stem Rules

**Origin Stems** (past tense — where the quality came from):
- First person, past tense, prompts a noun or short phrase
- "I learned this the hard way when ___"
- "My hands were steady because I had to be ___"

**Anchor Stems** (a person, place, or thing that grounds the quality):
- First person, present tense, specific noun anchor
- "The one I keep this for is ___"
- "I carry this from ___"

**Expression stems were rejected** — only Origin and Anchor stems exist.

**Writing box:** `border-bottom: 1px dotted #c8a96e` · 18px line height · fills remaining card height after effect text · full-width first line, shorter second line.

## Lucky Break — Template Reference (Pattern-Setter)

**Framework:** A · **Stem:** Origin · **Trigger:** "when luck could plausibly tip your way"

*When luck could plausibly tip your way,* narrate one small true fact about how it does — a door left unlocked, a face you recognize, a moment the odds turn.
Make a **Luck Check** — on a hit, the GM accepts the fact and may complicate it. On a miss, the GM narrates instead.

**Origin stem:** "I first noticed luck was on my side when ___"

**Locked decisions from Lucky Break session:**
- Flavor text dropped entirely — stem takes that space
- "GM accepts and may complicate" phrasing preferred over "must accept"
- Boost dice eligible on Luck Check

## Negation-Passive Problem (Always Flip)

Negation-passive effects cancel GM moves rather than create player rewards. Always flip to an active reward.

**Bad:** "You cannot be Rattled while this is in hand."
**Good:** "When you would be Rattled, choose one: **Shake It Off** — narrate why it doesn't touch you. **Use It** — accept Rattled and gain a Boost die on your next action."

This is the highest-priority revision type when auditing existing Boon drafts.

---

## Four-Scene Usability Test

Every Boon and every choose-one option must work in at least 4 of 5 contexts:
- Combat · Social · Exploration / dungeoneering · Wilderness / travel · Downtime

Ask: "If I'm robbing a train, does this work? Negotiating with a noble? Lost in a dungeon?"

## Boon Checklist

- [ ] Effect is Active OR Passive — never both
- [ ] Trigger sentence is one line, italic, ends before "Choose one:"
- [ ] "Choose one:" is on its own line above options
- [ ] Three options, each using CRPG verb syntax
- [ ] At least two options work across combat, social, and exploration
- [ ] Options are meaningfully distinct (different vectors)
- [ ] No negation-passive language
- [ ] Luck Check pill used if roll needed; none if no roll
- [ ] Origin OR Anchor stem — not Expression
- [ ] Stem is one italic sentence, past tense or anchor phrasing
- [ ] Writing box uses dotted rules, fills remaining card height
- [ ] Card_Key format: `card-name-boon`
- [ ] Ruleset: `base`
