# Writing Conventions

*Read this file before writing any card text — flavor, effects, stems, or triggers.*

---

## THE LOCKED RULES

These apply to every card in every session. Do not deviate without explicit Nathan confirmation.

---

### 1. Flavor Text
**Rule:** 2nd person · present tense · exactly 1 sentence · captures the emotional core of the card

✅ *"You don't have to do anything. You just have to be there."* (Bond — Warm Presence)
✅ *"You were not born with magic or ancient lineage. You were born with will."* (Ancestry — Human)
✅ *"You've kept people alive when the margin for error was exactly zero."* (Background — Field Medic, old version)
✅ *"You carry something ancient and unholy in your blood. It is not a curse. It is yours."* (Ancestry — Tiefling)

❌ "She has always been steady under pressure." (3rd person)
❌ "You were someone who kept people alive." (past tense)
❌ "Your character is driven by a sense of calm precision that comes from years of field experience and hard-won knowledge." (too long, too procedural)

**Flavor for Instincts uses universal text, not card-specific flavor** — the word in the header IS the personality. The universal flavor line is:
> *"True to their nature, your character is moved by this quality in this scene."*

---

### 2. Trigger Sentences
**Rule:** Italic conditional clause · soft gate · broad enough to fire in 4+ scene types (combat, social, exploration, travel, downtime) · does not over-specify the outcome

✅ *"When precision matters and the pressure is high,"*
✅ *"When your fiendish heritage — the unease you inspire, the fire in your blood —"*
✅ *"When raw power, momentum, or the force of your presence is what the moment needs,"*

❌ "During combat, when you attack an enemy:" (over-specified)
❌ "When you roll a 12 or higher:" (mechanical trigger, not fiction-first)
❌ "If the GM agrees that this situation qualifies:" (adversarial gate)

---

### 3. CRPG Verb Syntax (Choose-One Options)
**Rule:** `**Bold Verb Phrase** — brief fictional description` · verb is the player's signal to the GM · description hints at direction, never prescribes outcome · 3-4 words max for the verb phrase

✅ **Hold Steady** — steady those around you
✅ **Show Your Fury** — let your heritage surface; the room recalibrates
✅ **Find the Thread** — name what you share — the GM confirms whether they feel it too

❌ **Use your training to maintain composure and prevent the situation from escalating** (verb too long, outcome over-prescribed)
❌ Hold Steady: you gain advantage on the next roll (mechanical outcome prescribed)

**Three-vector design target** (where possible): one option affects self, one affects allies, one affects the scene/environment.

---

### 4. Origin Stems
**Rule:** 1st person · past tense · incomplete sentence designed to prompt a specific backstory memory · unique to each card (never generic) · completed with `___`

✅ *"I first learned what steady hands were worth when ___"*
✅ *"People expected less from me, and I used that when ___"*
✅ *"I stopped running from what I am when ___"*
✅ *"My blood runs ___ — I claimed it when ___"* (Dragonborn special — two blanks)

❌ "My backstory is ___" (generic)
❌ "I am this way because ___" (weak — doesn't trigger a specific memory)
❌ "You found this power when ___" (2nd person — breaks convention)

---

### 5. No Negation-Passive
**Rule:** Never write "you cannot," "you are immune," "you are not." Always reframe as active reward or altered state.

❌ "You are immune to fear"
✅ "Fear doesn't register the same way for you"

❌ "You cannot be charmed"
✅ "Something in you is already too old to be swayed that easily"

❌ "Other characters cannot manipulate you emotionally"
✅ "You've absorbed enough that very little lands the way it used to"

---

### 6. "This Scene" Not "The Scene Ahead"
The hand represents who the character IS right now — present emotional/instinctual state.

❌ "...in the scene ahead."
✅ "...in this scene."

---

### 7. Dismiss Conditions (Flaw cards)
**Rule:** Always offer two paths out: (1) character acts on the flaw, (2) an ally Cleanses it. Keep under one line.

✅ "You act on it, or an ally Cleanses it."
✅ "You let it go, exact the cost, or an ally Cleanses it."
✅ "Your position proves correct, is proven wrong, or an ally Cleanses it."

The `DISMISS` label uses small Cinzel uppercase, color `#8a3030`.

## BODY TYPE VARIANTS

Three patterns for card effect bodies, used across Background, Ancestry, and Ability cards:

### Choose-One
3 options in CRPG verb syntax (see rule 3). Standard for most Background and Ancestry cards.
Three-vector design target: self / party / scene.

### Luck-Check
Narrative trigger → `[Luck Check]` pill → GM adjudication. Used when wildly variable fiction is the mechanic (Gambler, Trade Road Courier, Halfling ancestry).

### Passive-Question (GM Question List)
3 GM-answerable questions in bullet format. Passive while card is held. Used when the power is information (Inspector Background, Elf Ancestry).
> *"The GM answers honestly."* — always included as a closing line.

---

## DICE MECHANICS TARGET

**20–40% of cards in any given set** should use explicit dice mechanics:
- `[Boost 2]` or `[Boost 1]` (inline teal pill)
- `[Luck Check]` (inline amber pill)
- Hit Die spend (inline purple pill)
- Reroll mechanic (described in text, sometimes with Luck Check)

The remaining 60–80% are pure fiction-first narration — no dice referenced.

---

## CONDITION VOCABULARY (canonical terms — use exactly)
- Rooted · Exposed · Rattled · Hidden · Sundered · Bolstered · Resolve · Break

**Removed:** Guard — eliminated as a keyword in June 2025. Do not use on new cards.

These are narrative vocabulary with no hard rules — GM adjudicates contextually. Never invent new condition names without explicit session approval.

---

## TERMINOLOGY (canonical — use exactly)
- **Crit** (not "Strong Roll" — retired)
- **Boost** (not "Strong Roll result" — retired)
- **Hit Dice** (not "HP")
- **Boost dice** (not "bonus dice")
- **Resolve** (temporary hit die layer at start of combat — replaces Guard)
- **Rally Tokens**
- **Pact** (Warlock economy)
- **Performance** (Bard)
- **Notoriety** (Rogue)
- **Debt** (Warlock)
- **scene beat** (not "beat")
