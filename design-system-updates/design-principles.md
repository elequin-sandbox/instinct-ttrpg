# Design Principles

*Read this file when evaluating new card designs or checking alignment.*

---

## CORE PHILOSOPHY

**The deck is who the character is in general. The hand is who they are right now.**

Cards are prompts, not rules. They frame fiction. They create collaborative narrative moments. They never fully prescribe outcomes — the GM and player fill the gap.

**Failure always moves the scene forward.** A miss is never a stop. The fiction advances regardless of the roll.

---

## FICTION-FIRST TRIGGERING

Cards trigger when the fiction supports it, not when a mechanical condition is met. Triggers are soft gates — the table's social contract handles edge cases.

The design goal: a player should be able to look at a trigger and immediately know whether this scene qualifies, without asking the GM.

---

## THE FOUR-SCENE USABILITY TEST

Every card must work in at least 4 of these 5 scene types:
1. **Combat** — direct physical conflict
2. **Social** — negotiation, persuasion, roleplay
3. **Exploration** — discovery, investigation, travel
4. **Rest/Downtime** — recovery, planning, interstitial moments
5. **Party Scenes** — internal group dynamics

If a card only fires in 1-2 scene types, it's too narrow. Redesign the trigger.

---

## THREE-VECTOR DESIGN (choose-one cards)

Where possible, design choose-one options to cover:
- **Self** — affects the character's internal state or capability
- **Party** — affects allies or the group dynamic
- **Scene** — affects the broader environment or situation

Not every card achieves all three. It's a target, not a requirement.

---

## THE NO-NEGATION-PASSIVE RULE

Cards never grant immunity or prohibit things. Every passive protection is reframed as an altered state or active advantage. See `writing-conventions.md` for examples.

---

## THE HAND AS PRESENT STATE

Cards in hand = the character's current emotional and instinctual state.
- Instinct cards represent a quality alive in the character RIGHT NOW
- Bond cards represent a connection active in this scene
- Flaw cards represent a pressure the character is feeling RIGHT NOW
- Background/Ancestry = past experience being called upon in the present moment

This is why flavor text is always present tense.

---

## CRIT REWARD RESTRAINT

Crit rewards ("draw a card," bonus effects) should only appear on ability cards when **deeply earned by the specific card's fiction**. Do not add Crit rewards to make a card feel powerful. Rarity makes them meaningful.

---

## CARD DESIGN CHECKLIST (before finalizing any card)

- [ ] Trigger fires in 4+ scene types
- [ ] No negation-passive language
- [ ] Flavor is 2nd person, present tense, 1 sentence
- [ ] Effect uses CRPG verb syntax
- [ ] Stem is past tense, 1st person, specific
- [ ] Text says "this scene" not "the scene ahead"
- [ ] No over-prescribed outcomes
- [ ] Dice mechanics target met (20-40% across the set)
- [ ] Three-vector design attempted (choose-one cards)
- [ ] Crit options are **thematically native** — no keyword insertion for synergy only
- [ ] Crit options: **2–3 max**, each **3–6 words**
- [ ] Failure line **moves the scene forward** — never "nothing happens"
- [ ] No passive debuff language on Flaw cards ("while held, [effect]") — hand slot cost IS the consequence

---

## TONE & VOICE

**Not a rulebook.** Cards should read like a whisper from a seasoned player to a new one — warm, confident, a little poetic. Never bureaucratic.

**Not over-defensive.** Card language should not feel like it's anticipating abuse. Trust the table.

**Fiction colloquialisms to avoid:**
- "the fiction advances" (designer language, not player language)
- "narratively" (same)
- "mechanically speaking"
- "as a spotlight character"
- "spotlight/turn order"

---

## THE GAME'S NORTH STAR (Playtest #1, Ben)

> *"The core of the game is giving people funny excuses to use their cards in unexpected ways."*

Every system decision should be measured against this. If a rule makes it harder to use cards unexpectedly, reconsider it. If a mechanic creates a funny excuse to use a card, protect it.

| Slay the Spire | Condition cards |
