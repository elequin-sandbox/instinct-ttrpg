## §1 — Objective setup & telegraphing script (P1)

Mechanic → `core-rules.md` §6 (Objectives & Challenge Pools). This section is the **script**.

At scene/Contest open:

1. Ask the table plainly: *"What are we actually trying to do here?"* Get a concrete answer — "steal
   the keys," "get out of the cell," "calm him down enough that he backs off" — and state it back as
   **the objective**, out loud, so everyone heard the same thing.
2. **Telegraph what success looks like** before any dice hit the table: *"If you clear this pool, X
   happens."* If more than one lane fits the fiction (persuasion vs. a strike), say so before players
   commit — don't make them guess which lane you had in mind.
3. Build the challenge pool sized to the objective (difficulty table, `core-rules.md` §6). Split into
   **waves** if the objective has natural stages, and telegraph each wave as it becomes relevant.
4. If a player pivots creatively mid-objective and it doesn't map to the current pool, **don't rule it
   doesn't count** — telegraph a smaller **declared sub-objective** pool live (S3, `core-rules.md` §6)
   instead.

**Why (Nathan, debrief):** *"The number one takeaway that is actionable is redefining how objectives
work… letting the players define what that is… instructing GMs to telegraph it way more."*

## §2 — Encounter design checklist (P1 + P3)

Before running any scene:

- [ ] Objective stated in plain language and telegraphed (§1).
- [ ] At least one alternate path considered, even if not offered outright.
- [ ] Scene 1 specifically scripted to reward one unexpected/"illegal" card use early (§3).
- [ ] Scene length budgeted — trim scene 1 so the table reaches a fresh hand draw quickly (§5 pacing).
- [ ] Enemy Threat and Hit Dice arrangement decided and ready to reveal upfront (`core-rules.md` §8).

## §3 — Scene 1 script: teach creative card use (P3)

**Problem:** new players read a hand literally ("all my cards are Barbarian punchy stuff") and feel
disarmed the moment the scene isn't a straightforward fight.

**Fix — script scene 1 to teach two things fast, before the first challenge pool opens:**

1. **Play off-card when your hand doesn't fit the scene.** A combat card can trigger in a social beat;
   a verbal card can trigger in a fight. The label is a suggestion, not a cage.
2. **Let your hand inform your character's mood**, not just their options.

**GM technique:** reward the first unexpected application hard — say yes, make it land, let the table
see it work. Then move to a fresh draw faster than usual; scene 1's only job is the teach above, so
don't let it outlast that job.

**One-line GM guide callout:** *"Scene 1's job is to teach: the label is a suggestion, not a cage."*

**Why (Joey, Playtest 3):** *"I felt kind of disarmed for a lot of the session… all of my things were
just Barbarian punchy stuff."*

## §4 — Threat / "trading blows" script (S1 — ⚠️ open, `[needs Nathan verify]`)

Not a locked rule — a drafted GM script pending confirmation. Mechanic pointer:
`core-rules.md` §8.

- Players understood Threat once it was explained, but **all-defend ("turtling") rounds** read as
  story-stagnant — no visible cost for ignoring the objective lane.
- **Draft GM script:** when a round goes all-defend, say so out loud and apply a cost — the objective
  pool gets one die harder next wave, or a parallel track (noise, reinforcements, the clock) advances.
  Make the tradeoff visible the same way Threat itself is visible.
- Possible rename/subtitle for player-facing copy: Threat as **incoming** or **trading blows**. Do not
  apply this rename to cards or the keyword table until Nathan confirms — `core-rules.md` still owns
  the keyword name.

## §5 — Pacing: more scenes per one-shot (S4)

Target **4+ scenes** in a one-shot so class-defining Core cards that barely fired in Playtest 3
(Barbarian Frenzy, Rogue Ace top-deck, extra Paladin Vow rolls) get room to show up. Budget the extra
time by trimming scene 1 to its teaching job only (§3) — don't cut later scenes to compensate.
