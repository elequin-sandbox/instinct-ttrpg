🎪 **PLAYTEST 2 — "THE PRINCESS AND THE PLEBEIANS"** 🎪

**The cast:**
🎩 **Trillion West Wind** (Dan) — vain warlock ringmaster, Fey patron named Oberon who talks down to him, secretly a softie
💪 **Cork** (Ben) — 275-year-old half-elf strongman who hunches and hustles people. Extremely jacked under the robe.
🎯 **Odin Hasselhoff** (Evan) — halfling rogue/barker, master of disguise, appeared from literally nowhere at least twice
🤡 **Bubbles** (Michael) — half-orc clown, slept badly, juggles. trust the juggling.

**What happened:**
The Stradivarius Traveling Circus rolls into town and puts on a show. Trillion conjures a real wind dragon. Cork sandbags his strongman act (for business reasons). Odin does a secret trapeze stunt as a "mystery stranger from the crowd." The king — disguised badly in the audience — is absolutely losing his mind.

Then three lions burst through the tent. Turns out they were possessed by an eldritch spirit named Dave, who is protein deficient and found Cork's whey protein. The party fights them off, saves the king, and gets hired to rescue his daughter — **Princess Peanut Butter Cup** — from a tower full of baby-ified Knights of the Round Table.

They wade through a cursed bog. Cork outruns an infinite magic staircase on all fours. Trillion calls Oberon to sing the babies to sleep with a targeted lullaby that psychologically destroys him. The door is locked. What follows is an extended sequence involving lost lockpicks, a thrown suit of armor, a smoke bomb, and finally Oberon just elbowing it open and leaving.

The babies wake up as ghost knights with swords. Bubbles catches the swords and **starts juggling them** in front of the knights. Two of them simply cease to exist out of confusion. Cork's childhood doll is magically decapitated. He takes emotional damage. His rage hits 6. He goes off. He turns into a Yeti and carries the princess, five babies, and Trillion's unconscious body down a waterslide staircase. Bubbles stays and juggles the falling rocks back into the walls. The tower is rebuilt. Everyone makes it out.

Sir Mance-a-Lot fires his squire and starts training feral men as cavalry steeds. The princess declines the circus invitation. Cork rages about it.

**What we talked about after:**

- **Dice inflation** — stacking aids made level 1 feel like late-game. fixing this by capping aid to one ally per action (no self-aid), then scaling DCs back down accordingly
- **Telegraphed threat** — the core combat loop (see incoming damage → choose to defend or push) felt great and is staying
- **Aid + teamwork moments** — best feeling in the game. "rolling 9 dice together" will remain the emotional peak, just tuned
- **Status effects (rattled/exposed/rooted)** — not doing enough work yet, getting cut for the next playtest
- **Bond cards** — trigger is too passive (you wait for a moment vs. creating one). rewriting so *you* initiate
- **Connection cards** — new card type idea: everyone's character name goes on a card, shuffled into each other's decks. draw someone's name → narrate a shared memory in the current scene → you both get a boost. testing this next
- **Two flaws** — one wasn't enough. adding a second flaw slot
- **Reactions** — hard to know *when* to trigger them under simultaneous threat. rewriting trigger language to match the new combat format
- **Barbarian rage balance** — cascading frenzy was funny in a one-shot, unplayable long-term. failures = ones only, frenzy capped at 4 dice
- **Everything-as-a-skill-check** — raised the idea of making every non-combat obstacle work like the group skill check (GM sets dice pool, players clear it). testing this
