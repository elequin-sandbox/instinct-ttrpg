THE STORY (Episode Recap)

The cast:

💪 Chotsky (Joe) — Barbarian brawler with a nickel-plated WWE gimmick; an old British gentleman energy except for the one supernatural farm-arm situation he's still processing

🔮 Briggs Pennyweather (Briggs) — Warlock strongman, bound to The Old One; one massive cursed arm and the manners of a man who lost the plot twenty years ago

⚔️ Wallace (Wallace) — Paladin wrestler powered by earnest Holy Light; vows, spectral sheep, and the urge to condemn anyone who swears in his presence

🎴 Jossie (Jossie) — Rogue with Ace up his sleeve — "I have been preparing for this exact moment" — and the patience to wait for it

What happened:

The annual Royal Royale tears up the Elm District — casinos, hay bales, and the biggest wrestling spectacle in the Flat Top Expanse. Prince Macho (fifteen, evil, fabulous) accuses the whole bracket of doping on Juice — Arcane steroids, presumably — and Arcane-gauntlets the party into custody before the bell even rings.

You wake in iron-barred cells. Guard Julia patrols with a heel-click that could cut glass. Briggs talks his way toward a baronet's visa fraud. Wallace unleashes a faith check backed by a vow and a spectral friendly sheep so unreasonably large the warden forgets his own name. Chotsky kicks the partition until the orc in the next cell punches through the wall (tag-team energy). Keys change hands. Juice still hums in your blood.

Then it's deadly contest time — Threat dice on the table, objective pools, defend-or-strike choices every round. Wallace throws his body between his friends and harm. Jossie lines up the hidden strike. Connection cards pull childhood playground trauma and sink-pipe sabotage into the middle of a prison brawl. Somewhere along the way the table decides two of you are married and that's just canon now.

You never quite made it to the crowning ceremony — Prince Macho's villain arc remains unpunched — but you absolutely made it to the part where Instinct RPG shows what it is: cards in hand, story at the table, dice flying, nobody playing it safe.

WHAT WE DISCUSSED (You Were Heard)

Your feedback is already driving the next build. Major threads:

🎯 Objectives need to be ours, not a mystery

Several of you expected to progress a clear counter — break out, get keys, soften a guard — and felt friction when the goal shifted or creative solutions (talking, stealing, sneaking) didn't map to the pool on the table. Takeaway: co-define the objective up front; telegraph what success looks like; use the same dice-clearing loop for social and combat scenes.

✨ Crits should punch the story, not bury the GM in keywords

You liked moments where a crit meant something visible — buff the team, shred the objective, protect a friend. Takeaway: a new flourish system — bold words on the card, you argue fiction fit, the number on the card is the effect. Less "what does Exposed mean this time," more "I Smite him and he drops to his knees."

🃏 Scene 1 should teach "use the card wrong on purpose"

Joey flagged feeling handcuffed to punchy cards outside combat — the system wants you to Dropkick a negotiation and punch a wall because your Barbarian's in a mood. Takeaway: first scene will deliberately reward one illegal, awesome card play; shorter opener; faster draw into the fun.

🛡️ Threat landed — but turtling can't stall the story

Threat dice created real "oh no" moments once the loop clicked. All-defend rounds that don't move the fiction felt flat. Takeaway: clearer cost for ignoring the objective lane; better names; GM tools so defense matters without parking the episode.

❤️ Connection cards are keepers

Flashbacks, marriage-at-the-table, sink-pipe assists — the relationship cards did exactly what they were supposed to do. We're keeping that energy.

📖 Primer self-teach worked

Reading the primer cold and asking each other questions gave useful signal on what's clear vs fuzzy. More walkthrough examples coming for first combat round and Threat.
